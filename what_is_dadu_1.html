What is a DADU?

Detached Accessory Dwelling Unit

Definition

A Detached Accessory Dwelling Unit (DADU) is a secondary residential structure located on the same lot as a primary single-family home. It functions as a complete, independent living space with its own entrance, kitchen, bathroom, and sleeping area, but it remains subordinate in size to the main residence.

How It Looks on Your Property

Primary Residence
DADU

Both structures share the same property lot

Key Requirements in Nashville

Size Limits

700 SF for lots under 10,000 SF; 850 SF for larger lots. Cannot exceed size of main home.

Height Limits

Cannot be taller than main house. Single story: 10 ft eave. Two story: 17 ft eave.

Owner Occupancy

Owner must live in either the main house or the DADU. Cannot rent out both.

Restrictive Covenant

Must record a covenant with the county before permit issuance.
