Did You Know?

Restrictive covenants are required to be filed with Davidson County before building any detached structure in Nashville. This includes DADUs, garages, sheds, and any accessory building with plumbing, HVAC, or electrical permits.

What is a Restrictive Covenant?

A restrictive covenant is a legal document recorded with your property deed that limits how a structure can be used. For detached structures, it ensures the building remains accessory to your primary residence and cannot be converted into an independent dwelling without proper permits.
