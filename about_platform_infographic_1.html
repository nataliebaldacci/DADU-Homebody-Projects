About This Platform

Homebody Projects / Nashville DADU

Why We Built This

In December 2025, Nashville changed the rules. For the first time, homeowners in most of the Urban Services District can build a Detached Accessory Dwelling Unit (DADU) on their property without special rezoning or overlay approval. 67,000+ parcels became newly eligible overnight.

But eligibility does not mean clarity. Homeowners still face basic questions: Can I actually build on MY lot? How big can it be? What will it cost? Who can help?

We built this platform to answer those questions before you call a contractor, before you hire an architect, before you spend a dime.

Who This Helps

Homeowners
  • Whether their parcel qualifies for a DADU
  • Maximum building size their lot allows
  • Whether restrictive covenants apply
  • What permits neighbors have pulled
Contractors
  • Identify high-eligibility neighborhoods
  • Understand permit requirements
  • See what competitors charge
Policymakers
  • Eligible parcels by council district
  • Permit trends over time
  • Geographic adoption patterns

Beyond DADUs

This platform also tracks Electric Vehicle Charging Station permits, another category that recently received regulatory clarity in Nashville. As home improvement regulations evolve, we will expand our coverage to help homeowners understand their options.
