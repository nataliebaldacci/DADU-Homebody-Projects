About DADUs in Nashville

Everything you need to know about Detached Accessory Dwelling Units

What is a DADU?

A Detached Accessory Dwelling Unit (DADU) is a secondary housing unit on a single-family residential lot that is separate from the primary residence. Also known as backyard cottages, granny flats, or in-law suites, DADUs provide flexible housing options for homeowners.

In Nashville, DADUs have been permitted since 2014 under specific zoning overlays and conditions. They represent an important tool for increasing housing density while maintaining neighborhood character.

Key Benefits

  • Generate rental income from your property
  • House family members while maintaining privacy
  • Increase your property value
  • Support Nashville's housing needs
  • Age-in-place flexibility
üë§
Owner Occupancy Requirement

The property owner must occupy either the primary dwelling or the DADU as their principal residence. A restrictive covenant is recorded with the deed to enforce this requirement.

  • Owner must live on-site
  • Covenant recorded with Register of Deeds
  • Runs with the land
üìê
Size Requirements
  • Maximum: 700 sq ft or 50% of primary dwelling
  • Minimum: 200 sq ft
  • Height: Cannot exceed primary structure
  • Setbacks: 3-10ft depending on location
üÖøÔ∏è
Parking Requirements

Generally, one additional off-street parking space is required for the DADU, though this may vary based on specific overlay districts.

Legislative Timeline
2014
DADUs First Permitted
Metro Nashville begins allowing DADUs in specific overlay districts through Specific Plan zoning.
2017
BL2017-835 Passed
Expanded DADU permissions and established clearer guidelines for construction and owner occupancy.
2020
COVID-Era Updates
Streamlined permitting processes and temporary fee reductions to encourage housing development.
2023
Accessory Dwelling Overlay
New overlay district created to expand DADU eligibility to more neighborhoods.
2024-Present
Continued Expansion
Ongoing discussions about further reducing barriers and expanding eligible areas.
