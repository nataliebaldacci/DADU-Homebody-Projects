Owner Occupancy Explained

Understanding the Residency Requirement

The Owner Must Live On-Site

Nashville requires the property owner to reside in either the main house OR the DADU. You cannot rent out both units while living elsewhere.

Owner Lives Off-Site, Rents Both Units
You cannot use a DADU to create a duplex investment property. The owner must reside on the property.

Why Does This Rule Exist?

Neighborhood Character

Keeps properties owner-occupied to maintain residential feel

Property Standards

Owner presence encourages better maintenance and upkeep

Community Investment

Owners living on-site are more invested in the community

Enforced Through Restrictive Covenant

The owner occupancy requirement is recorded in a restrictive covenant that stays with the property deed. This ensures compliance even if the property is sold. Learn more about covenants ↗
