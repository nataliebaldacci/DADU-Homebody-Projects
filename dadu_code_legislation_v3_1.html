3. HISTORIC ZONING DESIGN GUIDELINES

📋 About Historic Overlay DADUs

Properties within Historic Preservation or Neighborhood Conservation Zoning Overlays must comply with design guidelines reviewed by the Metropolitan Historic Zoning Commission (MHZC). Setbacks, size limitations, and design requirements are enforced by Historic. Conditions in § 17.16.030(G) restricting use and living space are enforced by Zoning. A Historic permit is required in addition to the Codes building permit.

10. METRO CODE SECTIONS

📖 DADU Zoning Standards Overview

The primary DADU regulations are found in § 17.16.030(G). A DADU is defined in § 17.04.060 as "clearly subordinate in size, height, and purpose to the principal structure," located on the same lot and detached from the principal structure. It can be an independent structure, a dwelling unit above a garage, or attached to a workshop or other accessory structure.

14. ERRATA & CORRECTIONS

⚠ Metro Website Citation Error

The Metro Nashville DADU permit information page contains incorrect code citations:

  • Incorrect: § 17.16.060(G)(7)(b) and § 17.16.060(G)(7)(c)
  • Correct: § 17.16.030(G)(7)(b) and § 17.16.030(G)(7)(c)

Section 17.16.060 addresses commercial uses, not residential/DADU provisions. The correct DADU standards are in § 17.16.030(G).

Additional Notes

  • Metro Codes does not maintain easement records. Check recorded plat, title abstract, land survey, or contact utility companies directly.
  • Metro Codes does not determine property lines. Obtain deed from Register of Deeds or hire a surveyor.
  • Contact Metro Stormwater when footprint is increased on parcel.
  • Contact Tennessee 811 at least 3 working days before digging.
  • If DADU was already permitted and renovations are needed, homeowner can self-permit for work up to $25,000.
